If you start with an odd number and count by twos, you will get only odd numbers. There is a range() function in Python that can do your counting for you. For example range(50) will give you all the numbers from 0 to 49; range(1,50) will give you all the numbers from 1 to 49; range(1,50,2) will give you all the numbers from 1 to 49 COUNTING BY TWO... in other words, all the ODD numbers from 1 to 49. What? You don't care about odd numbers, you need the even ones? You need to change only one argument in range(1,50,2) and then it will give you even numbers.

Oh, you want even numbers that go past 49? That's also a pretty small change. What do you think you need to change for that to happen?
